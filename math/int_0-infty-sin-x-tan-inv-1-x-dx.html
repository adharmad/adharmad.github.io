Integrate \( \int_0^\infty \sin x \tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{x}) \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\infty \sin x \tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{x}) \; dx \] Using integration by parts: \[ I = \int_0^\infty \tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{x}) \; d(- \cos x) \] \[ I = -\cos x \tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{x}) \Biggr|_0^\infty - \int_0^\infty (-\cos x) \; d(\tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{x})) \] \[ I = -(0 - \frac{\pi}{2}) + \int_0^\infty (\cos x) \; \frac{1}{1 + \frac{1}{x^2}} (-\frac{1}{x^2}) \; dx \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} - \int_0^\infty \frac{\cos x}{x^2+1} \; dx \] The solution to the second integral can be obtained from \( \int_0^\infty \frac{\cos x}{x^2+1} \; dx \) as: \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{\cos x}{x^2+1} \; dx = \frac{\pi}{2e} \] Hence, \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{\pi}{2e} \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2}(1 - \frac{1}{e}) \]
