Integrate \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 + tan^{\sqrt{2}} x} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 + tan^{\sqrt{2}} x} \; dx \tag{1} \] Using King's integral property, \( I = \int_a^bf(x) \; dx = \int_a^bf(b+a-x) \; dx \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 + tan^{\sqrt{2}} (\frac{\pi}{2}-x)} \; dx = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 + cot^{\sqrt{2}} x} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 + \frac{1}{tan^{\sqrt{2}} x}} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{tan^{\sqrt{2}} x}{1 + tan^{\sqrt{2}} x} \; dx \tag{2} \] Adding equations (1) and (2) \[ 2I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 + tan^{\sqrt{2}} x} \; dx + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{tan^{\sqrt{2}} x}{1 + tan^{\sqrt{2}} x} \; dx \] \[ 2I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} dx = \frac{\pi}{2} \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{4} \]
