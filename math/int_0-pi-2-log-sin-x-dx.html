Integrate \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin x)} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin x)} \; dx \] Using the half angle formula for \( \sin x = 2 \sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2} \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(2 \sin \frac{x}{2} \cos \frac{x}{2})} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} \; dx + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin \frac{x}{2})} \; dx + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\cos \frac{x}{2})} \; dx \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin \frac{x}{2})} \; dx + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\cos \frac{x}{2})} \; dx \] Substituting \( \frac{x}{2} = t \Rightarrow \frac{dx}{2} = dt \Rightarrow dx = 2 dt \), the limits of the both the integrals change from \( x = 0, x = \frac{\pi}{2} \) to \( t = 0, t = \frac{\pi}{4} \): \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\sin t)} \; 2 \; dt + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\cos t)} \; 2 \; dt \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} + 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\sin t)} \; dt + 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\cos t)} \; dt \tag{1} \]

Let us consider the second integral : \[ I_1 = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\cos t)} \; dt \] Substituting \( \frac{\pi}{2} - t = u \Rightarrow du = - dt \), the limits of the the integral changes from \( t = 0, x = \frac{\pi}{4} \) to \( t = \frac{\pi}{2}, t = \frac{\pi}{4} \): \[ I_1 = \int_\frac{\pi}{2}^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\cos (\frac{\pi}{2} - u))} \; (- du) = - \int_\frac{\pi}{2}^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\sin u)} \; du = \int_\frac{\pi}{4}^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin u)} \; du \] Since the variable in a definite integral is a dummy variable, \[ I_1 = \int_\frac{\pi}{4}^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin t)} \; dt \] Using this result in equation (1), we get: \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} + 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\sin t)} \; dt + 2 \int_\frac{\pi}{4}^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin t)} \; dt \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} + 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin t)} \; dt \] Again, since t is a dummy variable, we can substitute it with x, getting: \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} + 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin x)} \; dx \] Thus, \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} + 2I \] \[ I = - \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} \]
