Integrate \( \int_{-\infty}^\infty \cos (x^2) \; dx \)

The basic Fresnel integral:

\[ I = \int_{-\infty}^\infty \cos (x^2) \; dx \] Using Euler's identity, \( e^{-ix^2} = \cos (x^2) - i\sin (x^2) \). Thus, \[ I = \Re \int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-ix^2} \; dx \] Since \( e^{-ix^2} \) is an even function, \[ I = 2 \Re \int_0^\infty e^{-ix^2} \; dx \] Substituting \( ix^2 = t \), \( 2ix dx = dt \Rightarrow dx = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{it}} dt \). \[ I = 2 \Re \int_0^\infty e^{-t} \frac{1}{2\sqrt{it}} \; dt = \Re \frac{1}{\sqrt{i}} \int_0^\infty t^{-\frac{1}{2}} e^{-t} \; dt = \Re \frac{1}{\sqrt{i}} \int_0^\infty t^{\frac{3}{2} - 1} e^{-t} \; dt \] \[ I = \Re \frac{1}{\sqrt{i}} \Gamma{(\frac{3}{2})} = \Re \frac{1}{\sqrt{i}} \Gamma{(1 + \frac{1}{2})} = \Re \frac{1}{\sqrt{e^{i\frac{\pi}{2}}}} \frac{1}{2}\Gamma{(\frac{1}{2})} \] \[ I = \Re \; e^{-i\frac{\pi}{4}} \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\pi} = \Re \; (\cos{\frac{\pi}{4}} - i\sin{\frac{\pi}{4}}) \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\pi} \] \[ I = (\cos{\frac{\pi}{4}}) \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\pi} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{\pi}{8}} \]
