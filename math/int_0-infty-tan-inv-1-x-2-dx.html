Integrate \( \int_0^\infty (\tan^{-1} \frac{1}{x})^2 \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\infty (\tan^{-1} \frac{1}{x})^2 \; dx \] Substituting \( \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{x} = t \Rightarrow x = \cot t \Rightarrow dx = d(\cot t) \), the limits of integration change from \( x = 0, x = \infty \) to \( t = \frac{\pi}{2}, t = 0 \): \[ I = \int_\frac{\pi}{2}^0 t^2 \; d(\cot t) \] Using integration by parts, \[ I = t^2 \cot t \Biggr|_\frac{\pi}{2}^0 - \int_\frac{\pi}{2}^0 \cot t \; d(t^2) = (0-0) - \int_\frac{\pi}{2}^0 \cot t \; 2t \; dt \] \[ I = 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} t \cot t \; dt = 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} t \; d(\ln{(\sin t)}) \] Using integration by parts again, \[ I = 2t \ln{(\sin t)} \Biggr|_0^\frac{\pi}{2} - 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin t)} \; dt = (0-0) - 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin t)} \; dt \] \[ I = - 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin t)} \; dt \] Now we know that the solution of \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{(\sin x)} \; dx = -\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} \) from here. Hence, \[ I = - 2 (-\frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2}) \] \[ I = \pi \ln{2} \]
