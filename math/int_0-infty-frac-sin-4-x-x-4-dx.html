Integrate \( \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin^4 x}{x^4} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin^4 x}{x^4} \; dx \] In this integral, we will use the result from \( \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin^2 x}{x^2} \; dx \)

Using Feynman's technique of differentiating under the sign of the integral, let us first parametrize the integral: \[ I(a) = \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin^4 (ax)}{x^4} \; dx \tag{1} \] \[ \frac{dI}{da} = \frac{d}{da} \int_0^\infty \frac{4 \sin^3 (ax) \cos(ax) x}{x^4} \; dx = \int_0^\infty \frac{\partial}{\partial a} \frac{4 \sin^3 (ax) \cos(ax) x}{x^4} \; dx \] \[ \frac{dI}{da} = 4 \int_0^\infty \frac{sin^3 (ax) \cos(ax) x}{x^3} \; dx \tag{2} \] Differentiating one more time with respect to a, we get: \[ \frac{d^2I}{da^2} = 4 \int_0^\infty \frac{3 \sin^2 (ax) \cos^2 (ax) x + \sin^3 (ax) (-\sin (ax)) x }{x^3} \; dx \] \[ \frac{d^2I}{da^2} = 4 \int_0^\infty \frac{3 \sin^2 (ax) \cos^2 (ax) - \sin^4 (ax) )}{x^2} \; dx \] Now: \[ 3 \sin^2 (ax) \cos^2 (ax) - \sin^4 (ax) = 3 \sin^2 (ax) (1 - \sin^2 (ax)) - \sin^4 (ax) = 3 \sin^2 (ax) - 4 \sin^4 (ax) \] \[ 3 \sin^2 (ax) - 4 \sin^4 (ax) = \sin^2 (ax) (3 - 4 sin^2(ax)) = \sin^2 (ax) (4 \cos^(ax)-1) = 4 \sin^2 (ax) \cos^2 (ax) - \sin^2 (ax) \] Thus, \[ \frac{d^2I}{da^2} = 4 \int_0^\infty \frac{4 \sin^2 (ax) \cos^2 (ax) - \sin^2 (ax)}{x^2} \; dx = 4 \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin^2 (2ax) - \sin^2 (ax)}{x^2} \; dx \] Now we know from the above referenced link that: \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin^2 (ax)}{x} \; dx = \frac{\pi a}{2} \] and \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin^2 (2ax)}{x} \; dx = \pi a \] Hence, \[ \frac{d^2I}{da^2} = 4 (\pi a - \frac{\pi}{2} a ) = 4 \frac{\pi}{2} a = 2 \pi a \] Integrating both sides w.r.t a, we get: \[ \frac{dI}{da} = \int 2 \pi a \; da = \pi a^2 + c_1 \] From equation \( (2) \), we know that \( I^\prime(0) = 0 \). Hence \[ I^\prime(0) = \pi (0^2) + c_1 \] \[ c_1 = 0 \] \[ \frac{dI}{da} = \pi a^2 \] Integrating both sides w.r.t a again, we get: \[ I = \int \pi a^2 \; da = \pi\frac{a^3}{3} + c_2 \] From equation \( (1) \), \( I(0) = 0 \). Hence \[ I(0) = \frac{\pi (0^3)}{3} + c_2 \] \[ c_2 = 0 \] \[ I(a) = \frac{\pi a^3}{3} \] The value of the integral that we set out to solve is when we set a = 1: \[ I(1) = \frac{\pi (1^3)}{3} = \frac{\pi}{3} \] \[ \int_0^\infty \frac{\sin^4 x}{x^4} \; dx = \frac{\pi}{3} \]
