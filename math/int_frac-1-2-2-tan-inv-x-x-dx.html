Integrate \( \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\tan^{-1} x}{x} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\tan^{-1} x}{x} \; dx \tag{1} \] Substituting \( x = \frac{1}{t} \Rightarrow dx = -\frac{1}{t^2} \; dt \), the limits of integration go from \( x = \frac{1}{2}, x = 2 to t = {2}, t = \frac{1}{2} \): \[ I = \int_2^\frac{1}{2} \frac{\tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{t})}{\frac{1}{t}} (-\frac{1}{t^2}) \; dt = \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{t})}{t} \; dt \] Changing the variable of the definite integration to x, we get: \[ I = \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{x})}{x} \; dx \tag{2} \] Adding equations (1) and (2): \[ 2I = \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\tan^{-1} x}{x} \; dx + \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{x})}{x} \; dx = \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\tan^{-1} x + \tan^{-1} (\frac{1}{x})}{x} \; dx \] \[ 2I = \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\tan^{-1} x + \cot^{-1} x }{x} \; dx = \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{\frac{\pi}{2}}{x} \; dx \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{4} \int_\frac{1}{2}^2 \frac{1}{x} \; dx = \frac{\pi}{4} \ln{x} \Biggr|_\frac{1}{2}^2 = \frac{\pi}{4} (\ln{2} - ln{\frac{1}{2}}) = \frac{\pi}{4} (\ln{2} + ln{2}) = \frac{\pi}{4} (2\ln{2}) \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} \ln{2} \]
