Integrate \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin x}{1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x}} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin x}{1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x}} \; dx \tag{1} \] Using \( I = \int_a^bf(x) \; dx = \int_a^bf(b+a-x) \; dx \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin (\frac{\pi}{2} - x)}{1 + \sqrt{\sin 2(\frac{\pi}{2} - x)}} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\cos x}{1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x}} \; dx \tag{2} \] Adding equations (1) and (2), we get: \[ 2I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin x + \cos x}{1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x}} \; dx \] \[ I = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin x + \cos x}{1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x}} \; dx \] Substituting \( \sin x- \cos x = t \Rightarrow (\sin x + \cos x) dx = dt \), and changing the limits of integration from \( x=0, x=\frac{\pi}{2} \) to \( t=-1, t=1 \), and noticing that \( (\sin x- \cos x)^2 = t^2 \Rightarrow (1 - \sin 2x) = t^2 \Rightarrow \sin 2x = 1-t^2 \), we get: \[ I = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-1}^1 \frac{1}{1 + \sqrt{1-t^2}} \; dt \] Since the integrand is an even function of t, \[ I = \frac{1}{2} 2 \int_0^1 \frac{1}{1 + \sqrt{1-t^2}} \; dt = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{1 + \sqrt{1-t^2}} \; dt \] Further substituting \( t = \sin \theta \Rightarrow dt = \cos \theta d\theta \), and changing the limits of integration from \( t=0, t=1 \) to \( \theta=0, t=\frac{\pi}{2} \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 + \sqrt{1-\sin^2 \theta}} \; \cos \theta d\theta = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\cos \theta}{1 + \cos \theta} \; d\theta \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} (1 - \frac{1}{1 + \cos \theta}) \; d\theta = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} (1 - \frac{1}{2 \cos^2 \frac{\theta}{2}}) \; d\theta \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} (1 - \frac{1}{2} \sec^2 \frac{\theta}{2} \; d\theta \] \[ I = (\theta - \tan \frac{\theta}{2}) \Biggr|_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \] \[ I = (\frac{\pi}{2} - \tan \frac{\frac{\pi}{2}}{2}) - (0 - \tan 0) \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{2} - 1 \]
