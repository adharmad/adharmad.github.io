Integrate \( \int_0^1 \frac{\sin (\ln{x})}{\ln{x}} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^1 \frac{\sin (\ln{x})}{\ln{x}} \; dx \] Parametrizing the integral with an arbitrary parameter a: \[ I(a) = \int_0^1 \frac{\sin (a\ln{x})}{\ln{x}} \; dx \tag{1} \] Using Feynman's technique of differentiating under the sign of the integral, we get: \[ \frac{dI}{da} = \frac{d}{da} \int_0^1 \frac{\sin (a\ln{x})}{\ln{x}} \; dx = \int_0^1 \frac{\partial}{\partial a} \frac{\sin (a\ln{x})}{\ln{x}} \; dx \] \[ \frac{dI}{da} = \int_0^1 \frac{\cos (a\ln{x})}{\ln{x}} \ln{x} \; dx = \int_0^1 \cos (a\ln{x}) \; dx \] To solve \( \int \cos (a\ln{x}) \; dx \), let us substitute \( a \ln{x} = t \Rightarrow x = e^{\frac{t}{a}} \Rightarrow dx = \frac{1}{a} e^{\frac{t}{a}} \; dt \) and evaluate the indefinite integral: \[ J = \int \cos (a\ln{x}) \; dx = \int \cos t \frac{1}{a} \; e^{\frac{t}{a}} \; dt = \frac{1}{a} \int \cos t \; e^{\frac{t}{a}} \; dt \] \[ J = \Re \; \frac{1}{a} \int e^{-it} e^{\frac{t}{a}} \; dt = \Re \; \frac{1}{a} \int e^{-(it-\frac{t}{a})} \; dt = \Re \; \frac{1}{a} \int e^{-(i-\frac{1}{a})t} \; dt \] \[ J = \Re \; \frac{1}{a} (\frac{e^{-(i-\frac{1}{a})t}}{-(i-\frac{1}{a})}) = \Re \; \frac{e^{-it} e^{\frac{t}{a}}}{-ia + 1} \] Resubstituting \( t = a \ln{x} \), we get: \[ J = \Re \; \frac{e^{-ia \ln{x}} e^{\frac{a \ln{x}}{a}}}{-ia + 1} = \Re \; \frac{e^{-ia \ln{x}} e^{ \ln{x}}}{-ia + 1} \] \[ J = \Re \; \frac{e^{-ia \ln{x}}}{-ia + 1} \] Thus, \[ \frac{dI}{da} = \Re \; \frac{e^{-ia \ln{x}}}{-ia + 1} \Biggr|_0^1 = \Re \; \frac{1-0}{1-ia} \] \[ \frac{dI}{da} = \Re \; \frac{1}{1-ia} = \Re \; \frac{1}{1-ia} \frac{1+ia}{1+ia} = \Re \; \frac{1+ia}{1+a^2} \] \[ \frac{dI}{da} = \frac{1}{1+a^2} \] Integrating both sides w.r.t a, we get: \[ I = \int \frac{1}{1+a^2} + c = \tan^{-1} a + c \] Now we know from equation (1) that \( I = 0 \), when \( a = 0 \). Hence: \[ I(0) = 0 = \tan^{-1} 0 + c \] \[ c = 0 \] \[ I = \tan^{-1} a \] The value of the original integral to be evaluated is obtained by setting \( a = 1 \). \[ I(1) = \tan^{-1} 1 = \frac{\pi}{4} \] \[ \int_0^1 \frac{\sin (\ln{x})}{\ln{x}} \; dx = \frac{\pi}{4} \]
