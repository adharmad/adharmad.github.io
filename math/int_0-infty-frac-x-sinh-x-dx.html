Integrate \( \int_0^\infty \frac{x}{\sinh x} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\infty \frac{x}{\sinh x} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\infty \frac{x}{\frac{e^x - e^{-x}}{2}} \; dx = 2 \int_0^\infty \frac{x}{e^x - e^{-x}} \; dx \] Substituting \( e^x = t \Rightarrow e^x \; dx = dt \Rightarrow dx = \frac{dt}{t} \) and changing the limits of the integration change from \( x=\infty, x=0 \) to \( t=\infty, t=1 \), we get: \[ I = 2 \int_1^\infty \frac{\ln t}{t - \frac{1}{t}} \; \frac{dt}{t} = 2 \int_1^\infty \frac{\ln t}{t^2 - 1} \; dt \] Substituting \( t = \frac{1}{u} \Rightarrow dt = -\frac{1}{u^2} \; du \) and changing the limits of the integration change from \( t=\infty, t=1 \) to \( u=0, u=1 \), we get: \[ I = 2 \int_1^0 \frac{\ln \frac{1}{u}}{\frac{1}{u^2} - 1} (-\frac{1}{u^2}) \; du = 2 \int_0^1 \frac{- \ln u}{\frac{1-u^2}{u^2}} \frac{1}{u^2} \; du \] \[ I = 2 \int_0^1 \frac{\ln u}{u^2-1} \; du \] Using the result from \( \int_0^1 \frac{\ln x}{x^2-1} \; dx \), we know that: \[ \int_0^1 \frac{\ln x}{x^2-1} \; dx = \frac{\pi^2}{8} \] Hence, \[ I = 2 (\frac{\pi^2}{8}) \] \[ I = \frac{\pi^2}{4} \]
