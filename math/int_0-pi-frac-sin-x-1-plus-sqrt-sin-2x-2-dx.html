Integrate \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin x}{(1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x})^2} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin x}{(1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x})^2} \; dx \tag{1} \] Using King's identity, \( I = \int_a^bf(x) \; dx = \int_a^bf(b+a-x) \; dx \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin (\frac{\pi}{2} - x)}{(1 + \sqrt{\sin 2(\frac{\pi}{2} - x)})^2} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\cos x}{(1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x})^2} \; dx \tag{2} \] Adding equations (1) and (2), we get: \[ 2I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin x + \cos x}{(1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x})^2} \; dx \] \[ I = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sin x + \cos x}{(1 + \sqrt{\sin 2x})^2} \; dx \] Substituting \( \sin x- \cos x = t \Rightarrow (\sin x + \cos x) dx = dt \), and changing the limits of integration from \( x=0, x=\frac{\pi}{2} \) to \( t=-1, t=1 \), and noticing that \( (\sin x- \cos x)^2 = t^2 \Rightarrow (1 - \sin 2x) = t^2 \Rightarrow \sin 2x = 1-t^2 \), we get: \[ I = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-1}^1 \frac{1}{(1 + \sqrt{1-t^2})^2} \; dt \] Since the integrand is an even function of t, \[ I = \frac{1}{2} 2 \int_0^1 \frac{1}{(1 + \sqrt{1-t^2})^2} \; dt = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{(1 + \sqrt{1-t^2})^2} \; dt \] Further substituting \( t = \sin \theta \Rightarrow dt = \cos \theta \; d\theta \), and changing the limits of integration from \( t=0, t=1 \) to \( \theta=0, t=\frac{\pi}{2} \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{(1 + \sqrt{1-\sin^2 \theta})^2} \; \cos \theta d\theta = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\cos \theta}{(1 + \cos \theta)^2} \; d\theta \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{2 \cos^2 \frac{\theta}{2} - 1}{(2 \cos^2 \frac{\theta}{2})^2} \; d\theta = \frac{1}{4} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{2 \cos^2 \frac{\theta}{2} - 1}{\cos^4 \frac{\theta}{2}} \; d\theta \] \[ I = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \sec^2 \frac{\theta}{2} \; d\theta - \frac{1}{4} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \sec^4 \frac{\theta}{2} \; d\theta = \frac{1}{2} \frac{\tan \frac{\theta}{2}}{\frac{1}{2}} \Biggr|_0^\frac{\pi}{2} - \frac{1}{4} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \sec^2 \frac{\theta}{2} (\tan^2 \frac{\theta}{2} + 1) \; d\theta \] \[ I = (\tan \frac{\pi}{4} - \tan 0) - \frac{1}{4} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} (\sec^2 \frac{\theta}{2} \tan^2 \frac{\theta}{2} + \sec^2 \frac{\theta}{2}) \; d\theta \] \[ I = 1 - \frac{1}{4} \Biggl( \frac{\tan^3 \frac{\theta}{2}}{3 \frac{1}{2}} + \frac{\tan \frac{\theta}{2}}{\frac{1}{2}} \Biggr) \Biggr|_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \] \[ I = 1 - \frac{1}{4} \Biggl( \frac{2}{3} \tan^3 \frac{\theta}{2} + 2 \tan \frac{\theta}{2} \Biggr) \Biggr|_0^\frac{\pi}{2} = 1 - \frac{1}{4} \Biggl( (\frac{2}{3} \tan^3 \frac{\pi}{4} + 2 \tan \frac{\pi}{4}) - (\frac{2}{3} \tan^3 0 + 2 \tan 0) \Biggr) \] \[ I = 1 - \frac{1}{4} (\frac{2}{3} + 2 ) = 1 - \frac{2}{3} \] \[ I = \frac{1}{3} \]
