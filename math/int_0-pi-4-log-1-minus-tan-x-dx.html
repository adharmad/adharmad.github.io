Integrate \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(1 - \tan x)} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(1 - \tan x)} \; dx \] Using \( I = \int_a^bf(x) \; dx = \int_a^bf(b+a-x) \; dx \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(1 - \tan (\frac{\pi}{4} - x))} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(1 - \frac{\tan \frac{\pi}{4} - \tan x}{1 + \tan \frac{\pi}{4} \tan x}}) \; dx = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(1 - \frac{1 - \tan x}{1 + \tan x}}) \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(\frac{2 \tan x}{1 + \tan x}}) \; dx = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} (\ln{(2 \tan x)} - \ln{(1 + \tan x)}) \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(2 \tan x)} \; dx - \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(1 + \tan x)}) \; dx \] Using the result from \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(1 + \tan x)} \; dx \), we have: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{(2 \tan x)} \; dx - \frac{\pi}{8} \ln{2} \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{2} \; dx + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\tan x} \; dx - \frac{\pi}{8} \ln{2} \] \[ I = \ln{2} \left[ x \right]_0^\frac{\pi}{4} + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\tan x} \; dx - \frac{\pi}{8} \ln{2} = \frac{\pi}{4} \ln{2} + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\frac{1}{\cot x}} \; dx - \frac{\pi}{8} \ln{2} \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{8} \ln{2} - \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\cot x} \; dx \] From \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\cot x} \; dx \), we know that the value of the integral is the Catalan's constant. Hence: \[ I = \frac{\pi}{8} \ln{2} - G \]
