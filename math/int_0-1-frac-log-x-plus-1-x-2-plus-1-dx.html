Integrate \( \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \)

The Putnam Integral

Method 1: \[ I = \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \] Substitute \( x = \tan \theta \). Then \( dx = \sec^2\theta \; d\theta \). The limits go from \( x = (0, 1) \) to \( \theta= (0, \frac{\pi}{4}) \) \[ I = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\frac{\ln{(\tan \theta + 1)}}{\tan^2 \theta + 1} \sec^2\theta \; d\theta = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\frac{\ln{(\tan \theta + 1)}}{\sec^2\theta} \sec^2\theta \; d\theta \] \[ I = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\ln{(\tan \theta + 1)} \; d\theta \] Using \( I = \int_a^bf(x) \; dx = \int_a^bf(b+a-x) \; dx \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\ln{(\tan (\frac{\pi}{4} - \theta) + 1)} \; d\theta = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\ln{\Biggl( \frac{\tan\frac{\pi}{4} - \tan \theta}{1 + \tan\frac{\pi}{4} \tan \theta} + 1 \Biggr)} \; d\theta \] \[ I = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\ln{\Biggl( \frac{1 - \tan \theta}{1 + \tan \theta} + 1 \Biggr)} \; d\theta = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\ln{\Biggl( \frac{1 - \tan \theta + 1 + \tan \theta}{1 + \tan \theta} \Biggr)} \; d\theta \] \[ I = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\ln{\Biggl( \frac{2 }{1 + \tan \theta} \Biggr)} \; d\theta = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}(\ln{2} - \ln{(\tan \theta + 1)}) \; d\theta = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\ln{2} \; d\theta - \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}}\ln{(\tan \theta + 1)} \; d\theta \] \[ I = \ln{2}\; [\theta]_0^{\frac{\pi}{4}} - I \] \[ 2I = \frac{\pi}{4}\ln{2} \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{8}\ln{2} \]
