Integrate \( \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\cot x} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\cot x} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\frac{1}{\tan x}} \; dx = - \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \ln{\tan x} \; dx \] Substituting \( tan x = t \Rightarrow \sec^2 x \; dx = dt \Rightarrow dx = \frac{dt}{1+t^2} \), the limits of integration go from \( x=0, x=\frac{\pi}{4} \) to \( t = 0, t = 1 \): \[ I = - \int_0^1 \frac{\ln{t}}{1+t^2} \; dt \] Using integration by parts: \[ I = - \int_0^1 \ln{t} \; d(\tan^{-1}t) \] \[ I = - \left[ \ln{t} \tan^{-1}t \right]_0^1 + \int_0^1 \tan^{-1}t \; d(\ln{t}) = - \left[0 - 0 \right] + \int_0^1 \tan^{-1}t \; \frac{1}{t} dt \] \[ I = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{t} \tan^{-1}t \; dt \] The Maclaurin series expansion for \( \tan^{-1}t \) is: \[ \tan^{-1}t = t - \frac{t^3}{3} + \frac{t^5}{5} - \frac{t^7}{7} + ... \] \[ \tan^{-1}t = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n \frac{t^{2n+1}}{2n+1} \] Thus, the integral becomes: \[ I = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{t} \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n \frac{t^{2n+1}}{2n+1} \; dt = \int_0^1 \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n \frac{t^{2n}}{2n+1} \; dt \] \[ I = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{2n+1} \int_0^1 t^{2n} \; dt = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{2n+1} \frac{t^{2n+1}}{2n+1} \Biggr|_0^1 \] \[ I = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{2n+1} \frac{1^{2n+1} - 0^{2n+1}}{2n+1} \] \[ I = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1)^2} = G \] which is the definition of Catalan's constant.
