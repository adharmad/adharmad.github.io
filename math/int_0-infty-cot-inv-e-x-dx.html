Integrate \( \int_0^\infty \cot^{-1} (e^{x}) \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\infty \cot^{-1} (e^{x}) \; dx = \int_0^\infty \tan^{-1} (e^{-x}) \; dx \] Substituting \( e^{-x} = t \Rightarrow -e^{-x} dx = dt \Rightarrow dx = -\frac{1}{t} dt \), the limits of integration change from \( x = 0, x = \infty \) to \( t = 1, t = 0 \): \[ I = \int_1^0 \tan^{-1} (t) \; (-\frac{1}{t} dt) = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{t} \tan^{-1} (t) \; dt \] Using integration by parts, \[ I = \int_0^1 \tan^{-1} (t) \; d(\ln{t}) = \ln{(t)} \tan^{-1} (t) \Biggr|_0^1 - \int_0^1 \ln{t} \; d(\tan^{-1} (t)) \] \[ I = (0-0) - \int_0^1 \ln{t} \; \frac{dt}{1+t^2} \] \[ I = - \int_0^1 \frac{\ln{t}}{1+t^2} \; dt \] Now we know that the solution of \( \int_0^1 \frac{\ln{x}}{1+x^2} \; dx \) from here. Hence, \[ I = - (-G) \] \[ I = G \] Where G is Catalan's constant.
