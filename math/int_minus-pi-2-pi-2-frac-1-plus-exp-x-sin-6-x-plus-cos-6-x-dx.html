Integrate \( \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{dx}{(1+e^x)(\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x)} \)

\[ I = \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{dx}{(1+e^x)(\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x)} \tag{1} \] Substituting \( x = -y \Rightarrow dx = -dy \), and changing the limits of integration from \( x = -\frac{\pi}{2}, x=\frac{\pi}{2} \) to \( y = \frac{\pi}{2}, y=-\frac{\pi}{2} \), we get: \[ I = \int_\frac{\pi}{2}^{-\frac{\pi}{2}} \frac{-dy}{(1+e^{-y})(\sin^6 (-y) + \cos^6 (-y))} = \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{e^y}{(1+e^y)(\sin^6 y + \cos^6 y)} \] Since the integration variable is superfluous in a definite integral, we can also say: \[ I = \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{e^x}{(1+e^x)(\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x)} \; dx \tag{2} \] Adding equations (1) and (2): \[ 2I = \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{(1+e^x)(\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x)} \; dx + \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{e^x}{(1+e^x)(\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x)} \; dx \] \[ 2I = \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1+e^x}{(1+e^x)(\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x)} \; dx = \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x} \; dx \] \[ I = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-\frac{\pi}{2}}^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x} \; dx \] Since the \( \sin^6 x + \cos^6 x \) is an even function, we can write: \[ I = \frac{1}{2} 2 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x} \; dx = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{\sin^6 x + \cos^6 x} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{(\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x)(\sin^4 x - \sin^2 x \cos^2 x+ \cos^4 x)} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{\sin^4 x - \sin^2 x \cos^2 x+ \cos^4 x} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{\sin^4 x + 2\sin^2 x \cos^2 x + \cos^4 x - 3\sin^2 x \cos^2 x} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{(\sin^2 x + \cos^2 x)^2 - 3\sin^2 x \cos^2 x} \; dx = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 - 3\sin^2 x \cos^2 x} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{1 - \frac{3}{4} (2\sin x \cos x)^2} \; dx = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{4}{4 - 3 \sin^2 {2x} } \; dx \] \[ I = 4 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{4 - 3 \frac{1 - \cos {4x}}{2}} \; dx = 4 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{2}{8 - 3 (1 - \cos {4x})} \; dx \] \[ I = 8 \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{1}{5 +3 \cos {4x}} \; dx \] Substituting \( 4x = u \Rightarrow dx = \frac{du}{4} \), and changing the limits of integration from \( x = 0, x=\frac{\pi}{2} \) to \( u = 0, u=2\pi \), we get: \[ I = 8 \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{1}{5 +3 \cos u} \; \frac{du}{4} = 2 \int_0^{2\pi} \frac{1}{5 +3 \cos u} \; du \] \[ I = 4 \int_0^\pi \frac{1}{5 +3 \cos u} \; du \] Using Weierstrass substitution \( \tan \frac{u}{2} = t \Rightarrow du = \frac{2 dt}{1+t^2}, \cos u = \frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2} \) and changing the limits from \( u = 0, u= \pi \) to \( t= 0, t = \infty \): \[ I = 4 \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{5 +3 \frac{1-t^2}{1+t^2}} \; \frac{2dt}{1+t^2} = 8 \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{5 + 5t^2 +3 -3t^2} \; dt \] \[ I = 8 \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{8 + 2t^2} \; dt = 4 \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{t^2 + 4} \; dt \] \[ I = 4 \frac{1}{2} \Biggl[ \tan^{-1} \frac{t}{2} \Biggr]_0^\infty = 2 ( \tan^{-1} \infty - \tan^{-1} 0) = 2 ( \frac{\pi}{2} - 0) \] \[ I = \pi \]
