Integrate \( \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}+1} \sin(x - \sin(x - \sin(x - ...))) \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^{\frac{\pi}{2}+1} \sin(x - \sin(x - \sin(x - ...))) \; dx \] Consider the integrand: \[ \sin(x - \sin(x - \sin(x - ...))) = y \] \[ \sin(x - y) = y \] \[ x = y + \sin^{-1}y \tag{1} \] Then: \[ dx = (1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-y^2}}) \; dy \] Performing the substitution in equation (1), the limits of integration go from \( x = 0, x = \frac{\pi}{2}+1 \) to \( y = 0, y = \frac{\pi}{2} \) Thus \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} y \Biggl( 1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-y^2}} \Biggr) \; dy = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} y \; dy + \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{y}{\sqrt{1-y^2}} \; dy \] \[ I = \frac{y^2}{2} \Biggr|_0^\frac{\pi}{2} + \left[ -\sqrt{1-y^2} \right]_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \] \[ I = \frac{1}{2}(\frac{\pi^2}{4} -0) - (\sqrt{1-\frac{\pi^2}{4}} - 1) \] \[ I = \frac{\pi^2}{8} - \sqrt{1-\frac{\pi^2}{4}} + 1 \]
