Integrate \( \int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{-\ln{x}}} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{\sqrt{-\ln{x}}} \; dx \] Substituting \( \sqrt{-\ln{x}} = t \Rightarrow x = e^{-t^2} \Rightarrow dx = -2t e^{-t^2} \; dt \), and changing the limits from \(x=0, x=1 \) to \( t=\infty, t =0 \), we get: \[ I = \int_\infty^0 \frac{1}{t} (-2t e^{-t^2}) \; dt = 2 \int_0^\infty e^{-t^2} \; dt \] From the Gaussian integral, we know that: \[ \int_0^\infty e^{-x^2} dx = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2} \] \[ I = 2 (\frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2}) \] \[ I = \sqrt{\pi} \]
