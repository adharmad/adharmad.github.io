Integrate \( \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{(x^2+x+1)^\alpha} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{(x^2+x+1)^\alpha} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{(x^2+2\frac{1}{2}x+\frac{1}{4} +\frac{3}{4} )^\alpha} \; dx = \int_0^\infty \frac{1}{(x+\frac{1}{2})^2 + (\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})^2 )^\alpha} \; dx \] Substituting \( x+\frac{1}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \tan \theta \Rightarrow dx = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \sec^2 \theta \; d\theta \), and changing the limits from \(x=0, x=\infty \) to \( \theta=0, \theta = \frac{\pi}{2} \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \sec^2 \theta}{((\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \tan \theta)^2 + (\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2})^2)^\alpha} \; d\theta = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sec^2 \theta}{(\frac{3}{4})^\alpha ((\tan^2 \theta + 1)^2)^\alpha} \; d\theta \] \[ I = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} (\frac{4}{3})^\alpha \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \frac{\sec^2 \theta}{\sec^{2 \alpha} \theta} \; d\theta = (\frac{4}{3})^\alpha \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \cos^{2 \alpha - 2} \; d\theta \] \[ I = (\frac{4}{3})^\alpha \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \sin^{2 (\frac{1}{2}) - 1} \cos^{2( \alpha -\frac{1}{2}) - 1} \; d\theta \] Using the trigonometric definition of the Beta function: \[ B(p, q) = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{2} \sin^{2p-1} \theta \cos^{2q-1} \theta \; d\theta \] \[ I = (\frac{4}{3})^\alpha \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \; B(\frac{1}{2}, \alpha -\frac{1}{2}) \] Using the relation between the beta and gamma function: \[ B(p, q) = \frac{\Gamma(p) \Gamma(q)}{\Gamma(p+q)} \] \[ I = (\frac{4}{3})^\alpha \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \; \frac{\Gamma(\frac{1}{2}) \Gamma(\alpha -\frac{1}{2})}{\Gamma(\frac{1}{2} + \alpha -\frac{1}{2})} \] \[ I = (\frac{4}{3})^{\alpha -\frac{1}{2}} \; \sqrt{\pi} \frac{\Gamma(\alpha -\frac{1}{2})}{\Gamma(\alpha)} \]
