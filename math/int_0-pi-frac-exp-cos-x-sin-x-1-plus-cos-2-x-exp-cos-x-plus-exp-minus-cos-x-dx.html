Integrate \( \int_0^\pi \frac{e^{\cos x} \sin x}{(1+\cos^2 x)(e^{\cos x} + e^{-\cos x})} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\pi \frac{e^{\cos x} \sin x}{(1+\cos^2 x)(e^{\cos x} + e^{-\cos x})} \; dx \tag{1} \] Using King's identity, \( I = \int_a^bf(x) \; dx = \int_a^bf(b+a-x) \; dx \), we get: \[ I = \int_0^\pi \frac{e^{\cos (\pi-x)} \sin (\pi-x)}{(1+\cos^2 (\pi-x))(e^{\cos (\pi-x)} + e^{-\cos (\pi-x)})} \; dx \] Since \( \cos (\pi-x) = - \cos x \) and \( \sin (\pi-x) = \sin x \): \[ I = \int_0^\pi \frac{e^{-\cos x} \sin x}{(1+\cos^2 x)(e^{-\cos x} + e^{+\cos x})} \; dx \tag{2} \] Adding equations (1) and (2): \[ 2I = \int_0^\pi \frac{(e^{\cos x} + e^{-\cos x}) \sin x}{(1+\cos^2 x)(e^{-\cos x} + e^{+\cos x})} \; dx \] \[ I = \frac{1}{2} \int_0^\pi \frac{\sin x}{1+\cos^2 x} \; dx \] Substituting \( \cos x = t \Rightarrow -\sin x dx = dt \) and changing the limits of integration from \( x = 0, x = \pi to t = 1, t = -1 \), we get: \[ I = \frac{1}{2} \int_1^{-1} \frac{1}{1+t^2} \; (- dt) = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-1}^1 \frac{1}{1+t^2} \; dt \] \[ I = \frac{1}{2} 2 \int_0^1 \frac{1}{1+t^2} \; dt = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{1+t^2} \; dt \] \[ I = \Biggl[ \tan^{-1} t \Biggr]_0^1 = \tan^{-1} 1 - \tan^{-1} 0 = \frac{\pi}{4} - 0 \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{4} \]
