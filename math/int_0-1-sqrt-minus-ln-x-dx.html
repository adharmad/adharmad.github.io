Integrate \( \int_0^1 \sqrt{-\ln{x}} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^1 \sqrt{-\ln{x}} \; dx \] Substituting \( -ln{x} = t \Rightarrow x = e^{-t} \Rightarrow dx = -e^{-t} \; dt \), and changing the limits from \(x=0, x=1 \) to \( t=\infty, t =0 \), we get: \[ I = \int_\infty^0 \sqrt{t} (-e^{-t}) \; dt = \int_0^\infty t^{\frac{1}{2}} e^{-t} \; dt = \int_0^\infty t^{\frac{3}{2}-1} e^{-t} \; dt \] Using the definition of the Gamma function: \[ \Gamma{(n)} = \int_0^\infty t^{n-1} e^{-t} \; dt = (n-1)! \] \[ I = \Gamma{(\frac{3}{2})} = \Gamma{(1 + \frac{1}{2})} = \frac{1}{2} \Gamma{(\frac{1}{2})} \] \[ I = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2} \]
