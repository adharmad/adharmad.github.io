The \( \frac{22}{7} > \pi \) problem:

\[ I = \int_0^1 \frac{x^4 (1-x)^4}{1+x^2} \; dx = \int_0^1 \frac{x^4 (x-1)^4}{1+x^2} \; dx \] Substitute \( x = \tan \theta \). Then \( dx = \sec^2\theta \; d\theta \). The limits of integration go from \(x = 0, x = 1 \) to \(\theta = 0, \theta = \frac{\pi}{4} \). \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \frac{\tan^4\theta (\tan\theta - 1)^4}{1 + \tan^2\theta} \; \sec^2\theta \; d\theta = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \tan^4\theta (\tan\theta - 1)^4 \; d\theta \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \tan^4\theta (\tan^4\theta - 4\tan^3\theta + 6\tan^2\theta - 4\tan\theta + 1) \; d\theta \] \[ I = \int_0^\frac{\pi}{4} (\tan^8\theta - 4\tan^7\theta + 6\tan^6\theta - 4\tan^5\theta + \tan^4\theta) \; d\theta \] The following formulae are well known: \[ \int \tan\theta \; d\theta = \ln{\lvert \sec\theta \rvert} \] \[ \int \tan^2\theta \; d\theta = \tan\theta - \theta \] \[ \int \tan^3\theta \; d\theta = \frac{\tan^2\theta}{2} - \ln{\lvert \sec\theta \rvert} \] \[ \int \tan^4\theta \; d\theta = \frac{\tan^3\theta}{3} - \tan\theta + \theta \] \[ \int \tan^5\theta \; d\theta = \frac{\tan^4\theta}{4} - \frac{\tan^2\theta}{2} + \ln{\lvert \sec\theta \rvert} \] \[ \int \tan^6\theta \; d\theta = \frac{\tan^5\theta}{5} - \frac{\tan^3\theta}{3} + \tan\theta - \theta \] \[ \int \tan^7\theta \; d\theta = \frac{\tan^6\theta}{6} - \frac{\tan^4\theta}{4} + \frac{\tan^2\theta}{2} - \ln{\lvert \sec\theta \rvert} \] \[ \int \tan^8\theta \; d\theta = \frac{\tan^7\theta}{7} - \frac{\tan^5\theta}{5} + \frac{\tan^3\theta}{3} - \tan\theta + \theta \] Thus: \[ I = \displaylines{ \Biggl[ (\frac{\tan^7\theta}{7} - \frac{\tan^5\theta}{5} + \frac{\tan^3\theta}{3} - \tan\theta + \theta) - 4(\frac{\tan^6\theta}{6} - \frac{\tan^4\theta}{4} + \frac{\tan^2\theta}{2} - \ln{\lvert \sec\theta \rvert}) \\ + 6(\frac{\tan^5\theta}{5} - \frac{\tan^3\theta}{3} + \tan\theta - \theta) - 4(\frac{\tan^4\theta}{4} - \frac{\tan^2\theta}{2} + \ln{\lvert \sec\theta \rvert}) + (\frac{\tan^3\theta}{3} - \tan\theta + \theta) \Biggr]_0^\frac{\pi}{4} } \] \[ I = \Biggl[ \frac{\tan^7\theta}{7} - \frac{2}{3}\tan^6\theta + \tan^5\theta - \frac{4}{3}\tan^3\theta + 4\tan\theta - 4\theta \Biggr]_0^\frac{\pi}{4} \] \[ I = \frac{1}{7} - \frac{2}{3} + 1 - \frac{4}{3} + 4 - 4(\frac{\pi}{4}) \] \[ I = \frac{22}{7} - \pi \] Now we know that \( I > 0 \) since by inspecting the integral, we can see that both the numerator and denominator are positive, and the integral goes from \( x = 0 \) to \( x = 1 \). Hence \[ \frac{22}{7} > \pi \]
