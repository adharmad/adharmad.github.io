Integrate \( \int_0^1 \frac{\ln{x}}{x-1} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^1 \frac{\ln{x}}{x-1} \; dx \] Substituting \( x-1 = t \Rightarrow dx = dt \), and changing the limits of integration from \( x=0, x=1 \) to \( t=-1, t=0 \), we get: \[ I = \int_{-1}^0 \frac{\ln{(t+1)}}{t} \; dt \] We know that: \[ \ln{(1+t)} = t - \frac{t^2}{2} + \frac{t^3}{3} - \frac{t^4}{4} + \frac{t^5}{5} - ... = \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} \frac{t^n}{n} \] \[ \therefore \frac{\ln{(1+t)}}{t} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} \frac{t^n}{n} \frac{1}{t} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} \frac{t^{n-1}}{n} \] Thus: \[ I = \int_{-1}^0 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} \frac{t^{n-1}}{n} \; dt = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n} \int_{-1}^0 t^{n-1} \; dt \] \[ I = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n} \frac{t^n}{n} \Biggr|_{-1}^0 = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n^2} (0^n - ({-1})^n) \] \[ I = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n+1}}{n^2} ({-1})^{n+1} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{2(n+1)}}{n^2} = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^2} \] The integral thus has evaluated to the infinite sum of reciprocal squares which is the solution to the Basel problem: \[ \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^2} = 1 + \frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{4^2} + \frac{1}{5^2} + ... = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \] \[ I = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \]
