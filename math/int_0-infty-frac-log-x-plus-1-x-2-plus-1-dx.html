Integrate \( \int_0^\infty\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \)

Method 2: Algebraic substitution \[ I = \int_0^\infty\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx + \int_1^\infty\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \] \[ I = I_1 + I_2 \] where \[ I_1 = \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \tag{1} \] and \[ I_2 = \int_1^\infty\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \tag{2} \] We know the solution to the integral \( I_1 \) from \( \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(x+1)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \): \[ I_1 = \frac{\pi}{8}\ln{2} \tag{3} \] Substituting \( x = \frac{1}{t} \) to solve the integral \( I_2 \), we have \( dx = -\frac{1}{t^2} dt \) and changing the limits of the integral change from \( x=1, x= \infty \) to \(t =1, t = 0 \), we have: \[ I_2 = \int_1^0\frac{\ln{(\frac{1}{t} +1)}}{\frac{1}{t^2} + 1} \; (-\frac{1}{t^2}) \; dt = \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(\frac{1+t}{t}})}{t^2 + 1} \; dt \] \[ I_2 = \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(1+t)} - \ln{t}}{t^2 + 1} \; dt = \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(1+t)}}{t^2 + 1} \; dt - \int_0^1\frac{\ln{t}}{t^2 + 1} \; dt \] Since the variable of integration is fungible in a definite integral: \[ I_2 = \int_0^1\frac{\ln{(1+x)}}{x^2 + 1} \; dt - \int_0^1\frac{\ln{t}}{t^2 + 1} \; dt = I_1 - \int_0^1\frac{\ln{t}}{t^2 + 1} \; dt \] \[ I_2 = I_1 - I_3 \] where \[ I_3 = \int_0^1\frac{\ln{t}}{t^2 + 1} \; dt \] We also know the solution to the integral \( I_3 \) from \( \int_0^1\frac{\ln{x}}{x^2 + 1} \; dx \): \[ I_3 = -G \tag{4} \] where G is the Catalan's constant. Hence from equations (3) and (4): \[ I_2 = \frac{\pi}{8}\ln{2} - (-G) = \frac{\pi}{8}\ln{2} + G \] Finally, the value of the original integral is: \[ I = \frac{\pi}{8}\ln{2} + \frac{\pi}{8}\ln{2} + G = 2 \frac{\pi}{8}\ln{2} + G \] \[ I = \frac{\pi}{4}\ln{2} + G \]
