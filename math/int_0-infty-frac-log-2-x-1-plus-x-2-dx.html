Integrate \( \int_0^\infty\frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(1+x)^2} \; dx \)

\[ I = \int_0^\infty \frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(1+x)^2} \; dx \] \[ I = \int_0^1 \frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(1+x)^2} \; dx + \int_1^\infty \frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(1+x)^2} \; dx \tag{1} \] Let us evaluate: \[ I_1 = \int_1^\infty \frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(1+x)^2} \; dx \] Substituting \( x = \frac{1}{t} \Rightarrow dx = -\frac{1}{t^2} dt \) and changing the limits of the integration from \( x = 1, x = \infty \) to \( t = 1, t = 0 \), we get: \[ I_1 = \int_1^0 \frac{\ln^2 {\frac{1}{t}}}{(1+\frac{1}{t})^2} \; (-\frac{1}{t^2}) dt = \int_0^1 \frac{\ln^2 {t}}{\frac{(t+1)^2}{t^2}} \frac{1}{t^2} \; dt \] \[ I_1 = \int_0^1 \frac{\ln^2 {t}}{(t+1)^2} \; dt \] Since the variable of integration is superfluous in a definite integral: \[ I_1 = \int_0^1 \frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(x+1)^2} \; dx \] Substituting back in equation (1), we get: \[ I = \int_0^1 \frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(1+x)^2} \; dx + \int_0^1 \frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(1+x)^2} \; dx \] \[ I = 2 \int_0^1 \frac{\ln^2 {(x)}}{(1+x)^2} \; dx \tag{2} \] Now we know that: \[ \frac{1}{1+x} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^{n} x^n \] Taking the derivative of both sides w.r.t x: \[ -\frac{1}{(1+x)^2} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^{n} nx^{n-1} \] \[ \frac{1}{(1+x)^2} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} nx^{n-1} \] Substituting this in equation (2), we get: \[ I = 2 \int_0^1 \ln^2 {(x)} \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} nx^{n-1} \; dx \] Switching the order of the summation and integral: \[ I = 2 \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} n \int_0^1 \ln^2 {(x)} x^{n-1} \; dx = 2 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} n \int_0^1 \ln^2 {(x)} x^{n-1} \; dx \] Using integration by parts repeatedly: \[ I = 2 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} n \int_0^1 \ln^2 {(x)} \; d(\frac{x^{n}}{n}) = 2 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} n \Biggl( \frac{x^{n}}{n} \ln^2 {(x)} \Biggr|_0^1 - \int_0^1 \frac{x^{n}}{n} \; d(\ln^2 {(x)}) \Biggr) \] \[ I = 2 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} n \Biggl( (0-0) - \frac{1}{n} \int_0^1 x^{n} \; 2\ln{(x)} \frac{1}{x} \; dx \Biggr) \] \[ I = 4 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n} \int_0^1 x^{n-1} \; \ln{(x)} \; dx = 4 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n} \int_0^1 \ln{(x)} \; d(\frac{x^n}{n}) \] \[ I = 4 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n} \Biggl( \frac{x^n}{n} \ln{(x)} \Biggr|_0^1 - \int_0^1 \frac{x^n}{n} \; d(\ln{(x)}) \Biggr) \] \[ I = 4 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n} \Biggl( (0-0) - \int_0^1 \frac{x^n}{n} \frac{1}{x} \; dx \Biggr) \] \[ I = 4 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} \frac{1}{n} \int_0^1 x^{n-1} \; dx = 4 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} \frac{1}{n} \frac{x^n}{n} \Biggr|_0^1 \] \[ I = 4 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} \frac{1}{n^2} (1 - 0) \] \[ I = 4 \sum_{n=1}^\infty (-1)^{n+1} \frac{1}{n^2} \] Now in order to evaluate this sum, we use the infinite sum of reciprocal squares which is the solution to the Basel problem which tells us that: \[ \frac{1}{1^2} + \frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{4^2} + ... = \frac{\pi^2}{6} \] \[ I = 4 \Biggl( \frac{1}{1^2} - \frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} - \frac{1}{4^2} + ... \Biggr) \] \[ I = 4 \Biggl( \Biggl[\frac{1}{1^2} + \frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{4^2} + ... \Biggr] - 2 \Biggl[\frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{4^2} + \frac{1}{6^2} + \frac{1}{8^2} + ... \Biggr] \Biggr) \] \[ I = 4 \Biggl( \Biggl[\frac{1}{1^2} + \frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{4^2} + ... \Biggr] - 2 \frac{1}{2^2} \Biggl[\frac{1}{1^2} + \frac{1}{2^2} + \frac{1}{3^2} + \frac{1}{4^2} + ... \Biggr] \Biggr) \] \[ I = 4 \Biggl( \frac{\pi^2}{6} - \frac{1}{2} \frac{\pi^2}{6} \Biggr) = 2 \frac{\pi^2}{6} \] \[ I = \frac{\pi^2}{3} \]
